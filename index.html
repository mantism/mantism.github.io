Tricking / Martial Arts

Since the age of 5, I have been practicing the martial art of Taekwondo. I was and have always been inspired by the fictional characters of shows like Dragon Ball Z, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, Power Rangers, Naruto, and more. At 9 I started competing in the American Taekwondo Association (ATA) Tournament Circuit and fell in love with what is known as "Extreme Martial Arts" or XMA. XMA is a division where competitiors combined traditional techniques with flashy acrobatics in a choreographed form to music of their choice. To me it was the closest thing to being like characters such as Goku and Naruto. I strove to be the best that I could be and this drive to improve led me to my beloved sport of Tricking.
The most common definition of Tricking is "a combination of martial arts kicks with gymnastics flips and twists". But to me and many other trickers around the world, its a lifestyle. Thanks to Taekwondo and Tricking I have learned and applied valuable skills such as discipline and perseverance to everything I do in life.

The Tricking Wiki

Recently, I have applied my love for tricking to my love for web design. In my spring 2016 semester at Penn I took the course "Information Design & Visualization" (FNAR 337) where I learned the principles of data visualization. For my final project I chose to tackle my passion for tricking. I sent out a survey to the global tricking page on Facebook and with the results I created this website. I call it, The Tricking Wiki. It is a composition of many graphics I created with the answers to the survey using HTML, CSS, and D3.js.
